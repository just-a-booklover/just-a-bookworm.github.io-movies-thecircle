Plot: Mae Holland starts working in a company which deals with technology and social media. The company encourages Mae to give up her privacy and dedicate her life to the company...

Duration: about 110 minutes.

My opinion: I saw this movie because a friend recommended it to me, and I think it perfectly matches with a book I just read, "1984" by George Orwell.
They are both about a society somehow controlled with the technology, but there are no other similarities. The film shows how the technology is accepted and encouraged, the book is told from a point of view of a rebel.
I loved the movie because it gave me a modern point of view over the society-controlled-with-the-technology thing. I mean, Orwell wrote in the late 1940s, and his technology ideas are a little old.
Even though I love sci-fi books, I can't stand the movies: they're too fake, made at the computer... This one is "moderate": weird technologies in a everyday context. I liked "The Circle".
The end was stunning, I had foreseen something completely different and I have been upset by how it was similar to "1984": they're both unexpected and somehow coordinated. Until the very end I was waiting, and maybe because I got used of similarities with "1984", for an act of rebellion, I don't know. Until the very end I waited for something different to happen, but nothing. And when it really finished... I couldn't believe it! Is it already finished? Did it really go this way? It was wrong, weird, but I guess I liked it.
